SPECTRE is an artist collective born in Italy in 2011. Its founding members were Stefano Moscardini and Enrico Viola.
SPECTRE focuses on the acknowledgment that the act of experiencing art changes its audience, so art can be used to agressively accelerate cultural particles. SPECTRE uses direct interaction, a controlled level of shock and unusual media to maximize this effect. The super-villainesque metaphor embedded in the collective name refers to this cultural infiltration approach, where art can  be described as a weapon.
SPECTRE's language makes a heavy use of technology, using it both as a signifier and a signified. SPECTRE develops its own software, build its own hardware and constantly researches generative and protean media.


				

					

						

							

								Wenn du lange in einen Abgrund blickst
							

							

								type
								installation
							

							

								medium
								software
							

							

								desc
								a digital mirror investingating the act of staring at yourself: through this installation you can look at yourself just like you would do with any normal mirror, but you won't be able to see your eyes.
							

						



						

							

								Guardami
							

							

								type
								installation
							

							

								medium
								interactive portrait
							

							

								desc
								A living portrait where a girl is endlessly asking for attention (she screams "Guardami!", look at me in italian). As soon as someone actually looks at her, she turns her back to the audience, hiding from the people who replied to her cry for attention.
							

						



						

							

								Self Portrait
							

							

								type
								sculpture
							

							

								medium
								interactive electronics
							

							

								desc
								A robotic sculpture exploring the concept of collective identity: Self Portrait is a synthetic living organism built with objects emotionally linked to the artists and their families,  equipped with a vascular system containing the blood of both the artists. Using a system of sensors, it perceives the audience proximity and reacts with screams of fear.
Loyal to a close mimicry of the fragile condition of living beings, Self Portrait "bled to death" while being exposed  at Section Pigalle in Paris. 		
							

						



						

							

								Danse Neurale
							

							

								type
								performance
							

							

								medium
								light, sound, machines, living flesh
							

							

								desc
								Developed and brought on stage with Lukas Zpira, the central aspect in Danse Neurale is to bring flesh, plastic and steel on stage together, make them complementary and let them express together as if they were a single organism. Lukas’ body is equipped with a series of wireless sensors: an EEG headset, a breath microphone and a modified stethoscope; moreover two flesh hooks are inserted under his skin and connected to a winch. As the biometric signals are collected, they are sent to the software, where they are parsed in real time and used to drive both the audio and the visuals; when the winch starts to pull Lukas’ flesh, it generates a neurological response that, in turn, becomes a biomechanics feedback: two huge wings made with his own EEG graph grow from his shoulders, start to flap and lift his body off the ground.
							

						



						

							

								Isosmosi
							

							

								type
								performance
							

							

								medium
								light, sound, machines, living flesh
							

							

								desc
								Directly descending from Danse Neurale, Isosmosi is a collaboration with italian performers Mutant Squad. A flow of energies between the two main performers is represented using two different EEG headsets to drive music and the graphic representation of "energy bubbles" separately."
							

						



						

							

								Fukushima Mon Amour
							

							

								type
								installation
							

							

								medium
								light, sound, canvas, software
							

							

								desc
								An audio-visual installation where the japanese flag is transformed into an animated representation of the uranium atom. The electrons orbiting around the nucleus behave behave like tracks in a musical sequencer, sonifying the atom structure using samples of japanese instruments, played on a traditional japanese scale.
